Foundations Reflection

Throughout foundations, we have been learning how to fine-tune our behaviours and mentality to better absorb the information that we’re learning. In sprint 1 we learnt about:

Thinking like a web developer

Discovering our values and identification

Learning how to introduce ourselves

We first learned how to think like a developer. In this primer, we researched learning competencies specific to being a developer. This including things like understanding exactly what’s being asked for a project, planning your approach and breaking it down into small steps. Then in the revision phase, you should try to refactor your solution and then revise on the whole process.

Then we did some research into our own personal values and identity, turning it into a blog about ourselves.

Finally, in sprint 1, we introduced ourselves to our cohort, telling our fellow classmates a little bit about ourselves.

Learning Plan

In sprint 2 we created a learning plan. This learning plan we included what our intentions we’re in terms of a career path, talked about our strengths and limitations in terms of learning, what skills we would like to see developed at Dev Academy, a commitment to how we would manage our workload and how we would seek help if needed, what we expected for the facilitators and we were asked to plan ahead for any commitments outside of the course.

Emotional Intelligence

In sprint 3 we learnt about EQ. We learnt that having a high EQ index is very beneficial to programming, and specifically useful for working within a team. We learnt that having a high IQ isn’t necessarily what employers look for in tech, and that your interpersonal relationship skills are much more valuable.

Neuroplasticity and growth mindset

Listening and Looping

In sprint 4 we learnt about our brain and how we can manipulate our thought patterns to increase our capacity for learning. We then did some listening and looping exercises with our classmates where we called on discord and took turns talking about something whilst the other person listened without interrupting, and then they would say what they interpreted from what you said back to you and vice versa.

What I’ve learnt from the foundations core learning

Something that I’ve learnt about myself during these exercises is that I don’t particularly like talking about myself, which I don’t think is necessarily a bad thing, but it is valuable to understand yourself especially in a learning environment so that you can navigate your learning weaknesses and play into your learning strengths.

I was surprised that Dev Academy took this ‘learn how to learn’ approach, and also surprised at how effective it has been, especially in the revision aspect. Revising my work each step of the way has significantly helped me to retain the learning competencies that I have learnt so far.

I think that I was a bit apprehensive at first for some of the blog entries because I thought they were a bit pointless, which may have stopped me from getting the most out of the exercise. But looking back on it now I think that they did help a lot, and maybe I should have been more open minded.
