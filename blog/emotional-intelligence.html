Emotional Intelligence

While IQ is your academic intelligence, your EQ is your ability to work in a team, respond to others and regulate emotions.

Emotional intelligence consists of three main factors:

1. The Practice of Mindfulness. 2. Self-Knowledge and Self-Mastery 3. Creating Useful Mental Habits

Emotional intelligence is important to me for the same reason that it’s important to everyone:

Mastering your emotional intelligence things will vastly increase your productivity, your team-working ability and your overall mood.

Something I’ve done recently which has helped me to be more mindful, increase my self knowledge and self mastery and create useful mental habits is creating a daily planner. This has helped me to not only wake up earlier and take care of myself, it’s also helped me to be more productive and regulate my mood.

In an age where we have been sucked into the temptation of seeking quick dopamine hits in the form of instagram reels and other short-form media, I’ve found daily planning to be extremely useful. It takes this hard-wired need for quick thrills and weaponises it in a more productive way. Now instead of watching reels, I get to click one of my little ‘task completed’ buttons. On top of satisfying my urge to do something enjoyable, it also feels far more gratifying knowing that I have done something to improve myself.

Ontop of this, I have started journaling throughout the day. I use my gitHub reflections to reflect on my work, and I journal to get a better perspective on how I’ve generally been feeling throughout the day.

This helps me to keep track of my emotions and to be more mindful about how to regulate them.
