Mindset

Neuroplasticity is the brains ability to form new connections. By utilising our brains ability to create new connections and finding ways to promote growth, we can more easily create long lasting connections to help us improve at a certain task.

Neuroplasticity

For a long time it was believed that after a certain age your brains ability to grow and form new connections was relatively fixed, but new research has showed that the brain has the ability to expand and form new connections long after it physically stops growing.

Understanding neuroplasticity helps us as learners to realise that our brains have an outstanding ability to grow and adapt. By embracing this concept, we can approach learning with a sense of optimism, knowing that our brains have the ability to form new connections and and learn new concepts.

Growth Mindset

The growth mindset is the belief that anyone can learn new skills with perseverance and dedication, whereas a fixed mindset is the belief that people are born with innate skills that can’t be learnt by others.

A growth mindset prioritises putting effort into and embracing challenges, cultivating curiosity and embracing feedback. With the belief that we can create new skills without the need for innate talent, we can overcome our learning prejudices and find our true learning potential

In conclusion, Neuroplasticity and the Growth mindset are valuable tools that we can use to help promote our personal learning growth.
