My Values

I would say the most relevant ethical decision I’ve made recently is the decision to move back to Auckland after living in Wellington for 2 years. This was a very tough decision for me to make as I had just started developing deeper connections with all the friends I had made down there. One of the main reasons that I moved back up was to start up my old band again with my best friend Mina. In Wellington, despite my efforts, I didn’t have the same opportunities to pursue my creative endeavours, both in film and music. I was aware that relocating from Wellington would deeply sadden a couple of my friends, but in the end I did what was best for me, knowing that I could always visit them, if not move back down at some point later.

Now that I am here, I’ve been making music far more frequently and I feel like I’m finally getting to be creative again. It’s also been very nice to see my family again, along with a few of my high school friends that I haven’t seen in a long time. Looking back on this decision retrospectively, I can identify some of my main core values as being connection and creativity. Finding a balance between these things along side working to keep a roof over my head has been pretty much my life for the last 4 years since I graduated high school. I think that I made the right decision, and already so many more avenues to further both my working career and creative career have presented themselves to me.

My family has always been creative. Growing up, my mum and dad filmed weddings and made short films all the time which was a catalyst for my interest in film. I would always try to get involved as much as could, and that keenness never really faded. I was lucky to have a very supportive and loving family that taught me the value of nurturing and cherishing your relationships with the people you love.

When I made it to high school I picked up the guitar and found myself quickly and comfortably situated with the kids in the music scene. I formed a band with a few of my friends, we rehearsed, played gigs and had the time of our lives.

I hope that my creativity will manifest itself as a strength on my learning journey, helping me to think of ideas for projects and assist me with finding creative solutions to the problems that I face. I also hope to create bonds with my classmates and leave this course with new friends that I didn’t have before.

Something that I have struggled with in the past is procrastination. I sit here writing this on Saturday, when I promised myself I would have it finished on Thursday. I think it’s important to be honest with myself about it, instead of letting it manifest and feeling gross about myself for my lack of motivation. My ability to battle procrastination is fully dependent on my awareness of its existence. In the times where I find myself in a bout of productivity and motivation, I need to set foundations to make it easier for my future not-so-directed self to cope. Working collaboratively helps a lot, I hate to let other people down, which spurs me on when there are people relying on me.

A collaborative confliction that resonates with me most would be in the process of writing a song in a band. Things can get pretty heated when you have conflicting ideas in a creative project. Its easy to take constructive criticism as an insult, and I’ve found myself have many fruitless arguments in the past. Something that helped me navigate these situations is not taking things personally. Instead, I try to be very objective. Give everyone their space to create, and try and find a solution that works for everyone. Creative collaboration is inherently tough, considering everyone has their own taste and opinion, but there will always be a middle ground to settle on.
