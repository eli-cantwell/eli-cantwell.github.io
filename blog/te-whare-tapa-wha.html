Introducing Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health. The 4 dimensions are:

  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whānau (family wellbeing)

Lets use this wellbeing model to create a personalised wellbeing plan.

Things that I am doing to improve my taha tinana:

Going to the gym twice a week

Going for walks in the park

Things I am doing to improve my taha hinengaro:

Getting up early

Eating well

Following a daily schedule

Things I am doing to improve my taha wairua:

Listening to guide meditations when I go for a walk

Things I am doing to improve my taha whānau:

Having dinner with my grandma every night

If I make sure I stick to doing he things that I have laid out here, I will be able to maintain an overall balanced health both mentally and physically.
