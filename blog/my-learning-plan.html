My Learning Plan (Foundations)

My long term goal after completing this course is to have a job in tech, whether that be back-end or something else. I also want to further develop my coding skills and start to work on app and game development.

One of my strengths as a learner is that once I pick something up and learn it comprehensively I can find quite creative ways to implement what I’ve learnt. One of my flaws when it comes to learning is that I struggle with the “just-in-time” learning model. When I don’t understand every aspect of what I’m learning, even if it would become clear later, it really trips me up and I struggle to move on.

I would like to develop on my collaborative skills. I’ve worked in hospitality all my life but I have never really had a job where I was part of a team working on a specific project.

I have to remind myself to get work done first to free up time later in the week for relaxation. This is essential because it’s impossible to tell how long something is going to take you, the time boxes are really just an indicator. If you end up taking a day off mid-week you can very easily end up inundated.

Its important to let your facilitators and your workmates know if you are getting behind on your work, so that they know where you are at and possibly give you a hand. If I ever don’t understand a concept or find myself behind on work, I will use the Dev Academy discord to either get in contact with a facilitator or one of my workmates.

My expectations of the facilitation team is to help me to understand concepts that I am struggling with and to help me to debug certain problems that I can’t figure out how to debug myself.

My Learning Plan (Bootcamp)

When I leave dev academy I hope to use what I have learnt to get a job within the tech industry and I also hope to start game development as a hobby.

My biggest strengths in bootcamp will be my ability to work well in collaborative projects, but I am worried about not having as much coding knowledge as some of my classmates and potentially not being able to contribute as much as I would like. This can be fixed with revision and practice though.

My biggest non-technical challenge in bootcamp will be making sure I stay on top of things. This is where it becomes very important for me to plan out my week, so they I don’t end up with more work than I had anticipated at the end of the week close to the deadline.

A non-technical human skill that I would like to see developed at Dev Academy is to improve my leadership skills.

My expectations of the bootcamp team remains the same as my expectations for the foundations team. I hope that my facilitators will give me help when I need it, and that they will accomodate for a fun and accepting learning environment.

My expectations for myself in bootcamp is that I get the work done well in advance so that I don’t end up inundated with work, and that I maintain a healthy schedule.
